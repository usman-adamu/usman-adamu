About

I am a doctor trained in clinical medicine with seven years of post-qualification experience across general medicine, surgery, paediatrics, and mental health. I hold a Master of Public Health with Merit and have co-authored peer-reviewed research published in international journals.

I am transitioning into public health data analysis and epidemiology, with a focus on health inequalities, surveillance, and population-level analytics. This portfolio demonstrates applied epidemiological methods using real NHS data from the OHID Fingertips platform.

01

Health Inequalities in England: Deprivation & Life Expectancy

An epidemiological analysis of life expectancy trends across English local authorities from 2001 to 2020. Examines deprivation gradients by sex, healthy versus total life expectancy, and inequality persistence over two decades using linear regression and deprivation quintile modelling.

02

Mental Health Trends in England: Depression, Deprivation & Hospital Admissions

A longitudinal analysis of depression prevalence trends from 2010 to 2024 across English local authorities. Investigates geographic variation, deprivation associations, and gender-stratified hospital admissions among under-18s using time series decomposition and regression modelling.
